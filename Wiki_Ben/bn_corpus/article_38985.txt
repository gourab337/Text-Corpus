Meanwhile, sodium-iodide (Na/I) symporter pumps iodide (I−) actively into the cell, which previously has crossed the endothelium by largely unknown mechanisms. This iodide enters the follicular lumen from the cytoplasm by the transporter pendrin, in purportedly passive manner. In the colloid, iodide (I−) is oxidized to iodine (I0) by an enzyme called thyroid peroxidase Iodine (I0) is very reactive and iodinates the thyroglobulin at tyrosyl residues in its protein chain (in total containing approximately 120 tyrosyl residues). In adjacent tyrosyl residues are paired together. Thyroglobulin re-enters the follicular cell by endocytosis.
